    {% trans "Residual Risk Assessment Guide" %}
    {% trans "Purpose of This Section:" %}

    {% trans "This section is intended to assess the residual risk—the level of risk that remains after risk mitigation measures or controls have been implemented. Even after applying security controls, some level of risk usually remains, and it's important to understand whether that remaining risk is acceptable or requires further action." %}

    {% trans "What to Do in This Section:" %}
    • {% trans "Evaluate Residual Risk Level:" %} {% trans "Consider the effectiveness of the controls that have been put in place. Determine whether they have significantly reduced the original risk or only partially addressed it. Based on this, assign a residual risk rating (e.g., Low, Medium, High, Critical)." %}
    • {% trans "Provide Justification:" %} {% trans "Clearly explain why you assigned that particular residual risk level. Your justification should include: the types of controls implemented (e.g., technical, administrative, physical), their effectiveness in reducing the likelihood or impact of the threat, any limitations or gaps that still exist, and whether the residual risk is within the organization's risk tolerance." %}
    • {% trans "Consider Further Actions:" %} {% trans "If the residual risk remains high or unacceptable, indicate whether additional controls are needed, or if the risk should be escalated to higher management for acceptance." %}
    {% trans "Example:" %}
    {% trans "Residual Risk Level: Medium" %}
    {% trans "Justification: Although multi-factor authentication (MFA) and encryption have been implemented, the system still lacks regular patch management, which may leave it vulnerable to known exploits. Therefore, while the likelihood of unauthorized access has been reduced, the residual risk remains medium due to potential vulnerabilities from outdated software." %}
